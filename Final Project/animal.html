Grizzly Bear

Montana designated the grizzly bear (Ursus arctos horribilis) as the official state animal in 1983. Grizzly bears can be found in Glacier National Park and the mountains of northwest Montana. As an exercise in the Democratic process, more than 55,000 students in 425 schools participated in an election to select a mammal symbol for Montana. 74 different animals native to Montana were nominated, but after the students conducted primary and general elections (including posters, voter rosters, speeches, campaign buttons, bumper stickers, and party caucuses), the grizzly bear won over the runner-up (the elk).

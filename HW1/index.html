Upbringing

I grew up in a military family, with my father having served in the Army for 21 years. I was born in Gelnhausen, Germany while he served there, along with my younger sister who was born a year and half later, but we moved back to the States shortly after. We then lived in Kentucky and then Kansas, though I barely remember either. Most of my childhood memories come from living in Sanford, North Carolina, which we lived in for 5 years. Then we moved to Savannah, Georgia for 2 and a half years before we had the opportunity to move to Europe when my father was offered a job with NATO. So, my family had the chance to live in Rome, Italy for 6 months and Bydgoszcz, Poland for 2 years. After this, my father retired and we moved to Helena, Montana where I finished out high school there. This experience has greatly shaped much of my world view, and I am greatly appreciative of the fact that I have been able to experience so much of the world growing up and can consider myself much more culturally expereince than many of my peers, though I do not claim to know everything or be superior in any regard.
